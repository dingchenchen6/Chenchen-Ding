## ğŸ”¬ Research Interests

My research aims to understand and predict biodiversity change under accelerating anthropogenic pressures, and to support evidence-based conservation planning.

- **Climate change impacts on biodiversity**  
  Effects of long-term climate trends, temperature anomalies, and extremes (e.g., heatwaves) on species distributions, community composition, and extinction risk.

- **Land-use change & human disturbance**  
  Quantifying how land-use intensity, habitat modification, and socio-economic pressure affect species richness, abundance, and community similarity/turnover.

- **Trait-based and phylogenetic approaches**  
  Integrating functional traits and phylogeny to explain differential vulnerability, sensitivity, and resilience of species and communities under global change.

- **Macroecology & biogeography**  
  Large-scale spatiotemporal biodiversity patterns (range shifts, biogeographical turnover) and mechanisms maintaining regional biodiversity.

- **Extinction risk assessment & conservation prioritization**  
  Building quantitative, reproducible frameworks to assess risk and identify conservation priorities to inform management and policy.

- **Biodiversity monitoring & large-scale databases**  
  Synthesizing global/regional datasets (e.g., PREDICTS, GBIF, IUCN and trait databases) to address knowledge gaps and improve monitoring strategies.
